Getting Started with Computer Vision with OpenCV and Processing

This project is a practical introduction to computer vision using OpenCV and Processing.

Read the early draft

Introduction covers:
  • Why computer vision?
  • More Pixels Law
  • Capture, Filter, Calibrate, Track, Shape, Train, Combine
  • Follow in the Footsteps of Giants
  • What you should know about Processing
 
The World of Filters covers the following filters:
  • Threshold
  • Adaptive Threshold
  • Blur
  • Dilate and Erode
  • Histogram Equalization
  • Edge detection
  • Brightness/contrast
  • Region of Interest
The Calibrate section covers:
  • Barrel and perspective distortion correction
  • Calibrating a camera with grids
 
The Track all the Things section covers:
  • Deciding which detector to use
  • Color Tracking in HSV Color Space
  • Brightness Tracking
  • Feature Tracking
  • Background Subtraction
  • Running average of background
  • Motion Detection
  • Temporal Coherence for Blobs
  • Windowed search
  • Skin detection
  • Face detection
  • Contours and Lines
The Shape of Things section covers:
  • Point of Maximum Curvature
  • Convexity Defects
  • Top Pixel
  • Contour Winding Holes
  • Contour smoothing
 
The Training Program section covers:
  • Histograms for SVM (color, contour)
  • Histogram of Oriented Gradients for SVM
  • K-Nearest Neighbor
  • Neural Networks in OpenCV
 
The Projects section covers:
  • Kinograph
  • Overhead Robot Tracking
  • Food Cart Calendar
  • Text Rain
  • AR Marker Tracking
  • Playing card detection
